The PiClass Project

Raspberry Pi Travelling Classroom - The project goal is to provide a travelling computer lab with some robotics components using a Raspberry Pi.

The classroom can support half a class, ie from 10 to 12 students and can travel between school buildings in a suitcase on wheels!

Who is it for?

In the first round, the project is designed for primary Swiss classes located in the French speaking part of Switzerland. The workshop activities are also available for non profit associations working with young kids and teens.

We also have PiClass project in Brazil for summer 2015 designed for schools and non-profit association in São Paulo.

In the second round, we will provide our services all over the world between July and August. Depending on the community growth and needs, we will provide some instructions to teachers and help to setup a local PiClass lab.

How much it will cost ?

The Raspberry Pi micro computer and the electronics components we selected are low cost, energy efficient and were created for educational purposes. A single machine costs only $35, it lets us have a standard graphical desktop with educational applications that can play with robotics, create electronics circuits and teach programming.

Workshop duration

The duration of a workshop will depend on the budget of the school or the representative organization's needs and desires about the classroom goals and the school plan. The PiClass can take place over at least a morning and can last up to one year for a big project. In short, it will fit. The duration of a lesson is 45 minutes. Here are some examples:

  • Maths with LEDs or how to spot on a grid creating drawings in a single afternoon (2 lessons)
  • Introduction to the use of a computer and educational games: a full morning (4 lessons)
  • Building a robot, inc. soldering and programming: 8-10 lessons over the school year
  • Development of a weather station to be used in sciences: 1 morning, then 1 afternoon (6 lessons)

What will the children learn

Generally children will learn to use a computer properly, to manage their files in a structured way, to control educational games, explore the hardware and turn off the machine when finished. They will learn to implement an experimental, explorational and observational device.

Through the creation of a small website, children also learn to use the keyboard, create and manipulate images, and identify different parts of a website. At the same time they will improve their English writing skills.

Through small electronic assemblies or a grid of LEDs, we reach educational goals such as the description of a path in its familiar space indicating the points of departure, points of arrival and directions to take. So we can do math with leds!

PiClass comes with a bunch of free software especially designed for children from 4 to 10 years old. From a simple memory game to Python or Scratch programming students will discover, play and learn.

The Raspberry Pi

The Raspberry Pi is a mini computer the size of a credit card. It is supplied as a single mother card (without housing, power supply, keyboard, mouse and screen) with the aim to reduce costs and allow the use of recycled materials.

This computer is designed to encourage the learning of computer programming. However, it is sufficiently open (2 USB ports and one RJ45 1) and powerful (ARM 700 MHz, 512 MB RAM) to allow a wide range of uses.

Raspberry Pi website: raspberrypi.org

Code & documentation

PiClass documentation and records for students are managed from GitHub. Guides for teachers about the use of Linux and Raspberry Pi system are managed from SliTaz Hg repositories.

The complete guide on SliTaz and the Raspberry Pi is already written and can be found in the SliTaz ARM Codex: PiBook

In order to have a wide range of tools for young students, the PiClass project develops some example scripts, GUIs and is very actively involved in the SliTaz GNU/Linux distribution.
